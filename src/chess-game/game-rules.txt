CHESS GAME RULES AND SDL2/C++ IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATION

1. BOARD AND INITIAL POSITION

- The board contains 8 columns (files a-h) and 8 rows (ranks 1-8).
- White begins on ranks 1 and 2. Black begins on ranks 8 and 7.
- White's back rank from a1 to h1 is:
  rook, knight, bishop, queen, king, bishop, knight, rook.
- Black's back rank from a8 to h8 uses the same order.
- White has eight pawns on rank 2. Black has eight pawns on rank 7.
- The square at White's bottom-right (h1) is light.
- White always makes the first move. Turns then alternate.

Recommended array coordinates:
- board[0][0] represents a8 and board[7][7] represents h1.
- White pawns move toward decreasing row indexes.
- Black pawns move toward increasing row indexes.

2. GENERAL MOVE RULES

- A player may move only a piece of their own color.
- A piece cannot move onto a square occupied by another friendly piece.
- Moving onto a square occupied by an enemy piece captures and removes it.
- Except for knights, pieces cannot move through occupied squares.
- Every move must leave the moving player's king safe. A move that leaves or
  places that king in check is illegal.
- The two kings may never occupy adjacent squares because each attacks the
  squares surrounding it.

3. PIECE MOVEMENT

King:
- Moves exactly one square horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
- Cannot move onto a square attacked by an enemy piece.
- May castle when all castling requirements in section 5 are satisfied.

Queen:
- Moves any number of unobstructed squares horizontally, vertically, or
  diagonally.

Rook:
- Moves any number of unobstructed squares horizontally or vertically.

Bishop:
- Moves any number of unobstructed squares diagonally.

Knight:
- Moves in an L shape: two squares along one axis and one along the other.
- May jump over other pieces.

Pawn:
- Moves one empty square directly forward.
- From its starting rank, it may move two squares forward if both squares are
  empty.
- Captures an enemy piece one square diagonally forward.
- Cannot move backward or capture directly forward.
- May capture en passant when all requirements in section 6 are satisfied.
- Must promote when it reaches the last rank, as described in section 7.

4. CHECK, CHECKMATE, AND STALEMATE

- A king is in check if its square is attacked by at least one enemy piece.
- A player in check must make a legal move that removes the check. This can be
  done by moving the king, capturing the attacking piece, or blocking the
  attack when the attacking piece is a sliding piece and blocking is possible.
- A king is never captured.
- Checkmate occurs when the current player's king is in check and that player
  has no legal move. The checking player wins immediately.
- Stalemate occurs when the current player's king is not in check but that
  player has no legal move. The game is a draw.

5. CASTLING

Kingside castling:
- White: king e1 to g1 and rook h1 to f1.
- Black: king e8 to g8 and rook h8 to f8.

Queenside castling:
- White: king e1 to c1 and rook a1 to d1.
- Black: king e8 to c8 and rook a8 to d8.

Castling is legal only if:
- The king has never moved.
- The participating rook has never moved.
- All squares between that king and rook are empty.
- The king is not currently in check.
- The square crossed by the king is not attacked.
- The king's destination square is not attacked.

The rook may pass through an attacked square. Castling rights are permanently
lost when the king moves, or for a particular rook when that rook moves. A rook
returning to its original square does not restore castling rights.

6. EN PASSANT

- When a pawn advances two squares from its starting rank and finishes beside
  an enemy pawn, that enemy pawn may capture it as though it moved only one
  square.
- The capturing pawn moves diagonally forward into the square passed over by
  the two-square pawn. The two-square pawn is removed.
- En passant is available only on the immediately following move. Any other
  move causes the opportunity to expire.
- The capture is illegal if it would leave the capturing player's king in
  check, including by exposing a rook, bishop, or queen line.

7. PAWN PROMOTION

- A White pawn reaching rank 8 or a Black pawn reaching rank 1 must immediately
  be replaced by a queen, rook, bishop, or knight of the same color.
- Promotion is mandatory and is not limited by the pieces already present.
- The player may choose any of the four pieces; the pawn does not have to become
  a queen.
- A promotion may also capture a piece on the destination square.
- The move is complete only after the promoted piece has been selected.

8. DRAW RULES

Automatic/game-state draw:
- Stalemate.
- Dead position: neither player can possibly checkmate by any legal sequence.
  At minimum, detect these common cases:
  * king versus king;
  * king and one bishop versus king;
  * king and one knight versus king;
  * king and bishop versus king and bishop when all bishops are confined to
    squares of the same color.

Claim-based draw (recommended as a UI action):
- Threefold repetition: the same position occurs at least three times, not
  necessarily consecutively.
- Fifty-move rule: each side has completed 50 moves (100 half-moves) without a
  pawn move or capture.

For strict modern FIDE behavior, also implement:
- Automatic draw on fivefold repetition.
- Automatic draw after 75 moves by each side (150 half-moves) without a pawn
  move or capture, unless the last move gives checkmate.

A position is the same for repetition only when all of these match:
- Placement and color of every piece.
- Side to move.
- Available castling rights.
- A currently available en-passant capture opportunity.

Optional agreed draw:
- If multiplayer controls support offers, a player may offer a draw and the
  opponent may accept it.

9. GAME END CONDITIONS

The game ends when one of the following occurs:
- Checkmate: the checkmating player wins.
- Stalemate or another draw condition: the game is drawn.
- Resignation: the opponent wins.
- Time expiration, if clocks are enabled: the opponent normally wins, except
  the result is a draw if the opponent cannot possibly checkmate.

10. REQUIRED GAME STATE

Store at least:
- The piece type and color on every square.
- The side whose turn it is.
- Four castling-right flags: White/Black kingside and queenside.
- The en-passant target square, or no target.
- The half-move clock for the fifty/75-move rules.
- The full-move number, incremented after each Black move.
- Position-history keys/counts for repetition detection.
- The selected square and generated legal destinations for the SDL2 UI.
- The game status: playing, check, checkmate, stalemate, draw, resigned, or
  timed out.
- Promotion-pending state and the pawn's destination square.

11. MOVE VALIDATION PIPELINE

For every attempted move:
1. Confirm the game is still active.
2. Confirm the source and destination are valid board squares.
3. Confirm the source contains a piece belonging to the side to move.
4. Generate or validate the piece's movement pattern.
5. Confirm its path is clear when it is not a knight.
6. Apply special requirements for castling, en passant, or promotion.
7. Apply the move to a temporary board, including any special capture or rook
   movement.
8. Reject the move if the moving side's king is attacked on the temporary board.
9. Commit the move, update all state fields, and switch the side to move.
10. Generate the new side's legal moves and determine check, checkmate,
    stalemate, or another draw condition.

Important implementation rule:
- Generate pseudo-legal moves from piece movement first, then filter them by
  simulating each move and rejecting every move that leaves the friendly king
  attacked. Do not rely only on whether a piece is pinned or whether the king
  was already in check.

12. SDL2 INPUT AND DISPLAY BEHAVIOR

- Convert mouse coordinates to board coordinates only when the click is inside
  the board rectangle.
- First valid click selects a piece belonging to the current player.
- Display only fully legal destination squares for the selected piece.
- Clicking a legal destination performs the move.
- Clicking another friendly piece changes the selection.
- Clicking outside the board or pressing Escape may clear the selection.
- During promotion, block normal board input and display four promotion choices.
- Visually distinguish the selected square, legal moves, last move, checked
  king, and captured-piece destinations.
- Once the game ends, reject further board moves but allow restart, quit, and
  review controls.

13. RECOMMENDED CORE TYPES

- enum class Color { White, Black };
- enum class PieceType { None, Pawn, Knight, Bishop, Rook, Queen, King };
- struct Piece { PieceType type; Color color; };
- struct Square { int row; int col; };
- struct Move {
    Square from;
    Square to;
    PieceType promotion;
    bool isCastle;
    bool isEnPassant;
  };

Keep chess logic independent from SDL2 rendering. The rules engine should accept
a game state and move, report whether the move is legal, and return the updated
state. SDL2 should handle windows, textures, input, animation, sound, and drawing,
but should not decide whether a chess move is legal.
